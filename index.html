The Cottage

196 Droman has been in the Ross/Anderson-Wallace family for more than 40 years, and has long been a refuge for tired souls seeking peace, beautiful scenery and magically fresh air. It is a much loved family home — very comfortable and well loved, but slightly faded around the edges — and it is extremely well adapted to take account of the vagaries of the Scottish weather.

The Location

"As far north-west as you can get on this island — you really can't get much further away from it all."

Droman is a small hamlet on Scotland's north-western coast, near Kinlochbervie in Sutherland. A rocky bay and pier are a two-minute walk from the door; sandy beaches are five minutes across the hills.

The wider landscape is moorlands, lochans, and bare rocky mountains. Northwest Sutherland is one of the most sparsely populated and hauntingly beautiful places in Britain.

Getting Here

Take the A894 to Kinlochbervie, then follow signs for Balchrick. Turn left towards Droman pier and the blue and white cottage is down the hill next to the track — you're as far north-west as it gets

Activities

The area rewards those who love the outdoors — from mountain walks and sea-bird watching to sandy beaches and fresh seafood.

Beaches

Polin beach is a short walk from the door. Droman Bay is a charming rocky cove at the end of the road. Oldshoremore and Sheigra can be reached on foot or by road. Remote Sandwood Bay is an 8-mile round trip and stunningly beautiful.

Walking & Climbing

Ben Stack, Arkle and Foinaven rise from the moorland nearby — Foinaven is the most challenging, mostly because of the trek to reach its base. Walk Highlands has good route guides.

Fishing

Sea fishing requires no permit. For loch, river and fly fishing, permits are available through the Durness & KLB Angling Club. Sea-angling equipment is in the workshop.

Handa Island

A Scottish Wildlife Trust reserve home to over 100,000 breeding seabirds — guillemots, razorbills, puffins, great skuas and more. Seasonal ferry from Tarbert; 3.5-mile circular walk around the island.

Durness & Balnakeil

Smoo Cave, one of the largest cave entrances in Britain, is just east of Durness. Balnakeil Craft Village has workshops, a gallery and the excellent Cocoa Mountain chocolate shop. The Faraid Head walk (approx. 5 miles) offers whale, dolphin and otter sightings.

Lotte Glob

Renowned ceramic artist with a wonderful croft garden and sculptures on the road along Loch Eribol out of Durness. Book a visit in advance.

Kylesku

Seasonal boat trips from Kylesku to Eas a Chual Aluinn — Britain's tallest waterfall at 200m. Also a chance to see seals and sea eagles.

Eating Out

The Quay House in Kinlochbervie harbour is recommended for coffee and lunch. Loch Clash Larder (a van past London Stores) serves fresh local seafood April–October. Further afield: the Crofter's Kitchen in Scourie and the Shorehouse Seafood Restaurant in Tarbert.
