The Cottage

196 Droman has been in the Ross/Anderson-Wallace family for more than 40 years, and has long been a refuge for tired souls seeking peace, beautiful scenery and magically fresh air. The cottage is a much loved family holiday home, not a holiday rental cottage, so the decor could be considered a bit faded in places, but it is extremely comfortable and well adapted to take account of the vagaries of the Scottish weather.

The Location

"As far north-west as you can get on this island — you really can't get much further away from it all."

Droman is a small hamlet on Scotland's north-western coast, near Balchrick in Sutherland. A rocky bay and pier are a two-minute walk from the door; sandy beaches are five minutes across the hills.

The wider landscape is moorlands, lochans, and bare rocky mountains. Northwest Sutherland is one of the most sparsely populated and hauntingly beautiful places in Britain.

Activities

The area rewards those who love the outdoors — from mountain walks to sea-bird watching.

Walking & Climbing

Foinaven, Arkle, and Stack rise from the moorland nearby. The 8-mile walk to remote Sandwood Bay is a highlight of any stay.

Fishing

Salt-water fishing requires no permit. Freshwater permits are available through Scourie Angling — details at the cottage.

Handa Island

Internationally famous for its seabird colonies — Britain's largest guillemot breeding population, plus puffins. Ferry from Tarbet.

Cape Wrath & Durness

40 minutes north: beaches, Smoo Cave, Cape Wrath lighthouse, and Balnakeil Craft Village.

Kylesku

Boat trips to see seals and eagles, with a view of Europe's tallest waterfall.

Kayaking

Two full-size sea kayaks with life jackets are available at the cottage on request, at your own risk.
